1\. Introduction
----------------

**Date of Issue:** 1997-11-28

**Date of Modification:** 2004-05-14

**Application:** This is an order that applies to officers and non-commissioned members of the Canadian Armed Forces (CAF members).

**Note -** Employees of the Department of National Defence (DND) are subject to the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat policy entitled _Smoking in the workplace_.

**Approval Authority:** This DAOD is issued under the authority of the Assistant Deputy Minister (Finance and Corporate Services) (ADM(Fin-CS)).

**Enquiries:** Director Strategic Corporate Services (DSCS)

2\. Standard Practice
---------------------

### Context

**2.1** The Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS) has published a comprehensive policy on smoking in the workplace that applies to public servants, including DND employees. This policy is designed to provide a healthier, safer work environment that is free, to the extent possible, of tobacco smoke.

**2.2** In keeping with the objectives of the TBS policy, a requirement exists for a similar instruction that applies to CAF members.

### Prohibition

**2.3** Smoking is prohibited in the workplace and smoking rooms may not be designated.

**2.4** The workplace is any indoor or enclosed place controlled by the DND or the CAF where DND employees or CAF members work or perform duties, including any:

1.  mess;
2.  adjacent corridor;
3.  lobby;
4.  stairwell;
5.  elevator;
6.  cafeteria;
7.  washroom; or
8.  other area used by DND employees or CAF members in the course of work-related duties.

### Serving with Outside Organizations

**2.5** CAF members who serve with an outside organization, e.g., the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO), United Nations (UN) or North American Aerospace Defence Command (NORAD), and are in a building or area controlled by that organization, shall comply with the smoking policy of that organization.

3\. Responsibilities
--------------------

### Responsibility Table

**3.1** The following table outlines the responsibilities associated with the implementation of this order:

| The … | is or are responsible for ensuring that this DAOD is applied … |
| --- | --- |
| Chief of the Land Staff | in combat vehicles and under field operating conditions. |
| Chief of the Air Staff | on military aircraft. |
| Chief of the Maritime Staff | inside ships and auxiliary vessels. |
| base and station commanders, and heads of establishments | in workplaces that fall within their area of responsibility. |
| CAF members | by making certain that visitors and clients refrain from smoking in the workplace. |
